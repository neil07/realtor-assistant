# 访谈记录：Natalie（Real Estate Agent）

**日期：** 2026-03-07  
**时长：** ~20分钟  
**参与者：** Helen（Clark）、Julian（俊峰）、Neil、Natalie  
**受访者背景：** Coldwell Banker 经纪人，从业约1.5年，兼职（另有会计工作），已成交7-8笔

---

## Part 1: 背景与获客

**Helen:** OK, let's begin. Hi Natalie, we just have a few questions to ask to help us understand the details of the solo agents. So would you mind telling us a little bit about your past year — like how many transactions have you closed in the previous one year?

**Natalie:** So I've been in real estate for a year and a half. I closed 7 transactions in about a year, year and a half.

**Helen:** Thank you. And how do you usually find your clients — like from referrals, or from social media, or from some third-party platforms?

**Natalie:** Third-party platforms.

**Helen:** Would you mind telling us a little bit about the third-party platforms?

**Natalie:** I would mind, because if I tell people they'll go get them from the same platforms that I would get them from, and then my prices go up. So if I tell my agent friends how I'm getting my leads, they'll go buy them too, and the prices are very steep. I spend about $4,000 every 6 months.

**Helen:** $4,000 every 6 months, like half a year, right?

**Natalie:** Yeah, to buy the leads.

**Helen:** And so how many of the clients that you have successfully closed deals — are from this kind of third-party platforms?

**Natalie:** 75%, I would say. I can actually give you an accurate figure instead of a guess — I'm getting my phone to look up how many. Four out of eight, because I close escrow next week on a deal, so it'll be eight instead of seven. And four of those were from the lead gen platform that I used.

**Helen:** OK, how about the rest — are they from referrals?

**Natalie:** No, the rest of them were family. My sister — she sold her house and then bought a new one with me. Family friends of my dad sold a duplex with me.

**Helen:** Sphere — the people that you're close with, like your family and friends — they're called your "sphere," like your sphere of influence. A lot of realtors get clients through that.

## Part 2: 营销支出与社媒

**Helen:** So apart from paying for the leads, what else would you pay for? Like would you pay for marketing?

**Natalie:** Yeah, I mean I've had opportunities to have people manage my social media and stuff, and the prices were high. And I don't know how soon I would obtain a lead from like Facebook or Instagram. I do have people connecting with me on there. But for me it would be more like help with my CRM — like my people's phone numbers, emails — it's like an Excel spreadsheet. The CRM is your people you send out emails to and stuff. But I just manage it on my own.

There's like — oftentimes I'll have like "oh I need to enter 10 people into that list" and then I just put it off because it takes me like — it's so quick to do and then I'll be like "okay now I have to add 20 people."

But all the social media offers I got, they were all like $300 a month. And then one time I liked this girl's content and I was like "how about like $100" and she — we couldn't find an agreement. And even at that, that's pretty steep.

I have an SEO guy too, and his numbers were around the same. It's hard for me to be willing to spend the money without seeing a return. The reason I buy my leads is because anytime I bought the leads in a six-month time frame for the $4,000, I turned it into a minimum of $15,000 in that six-month time frame. So it's a no-brainer to do that.

But with purchasing assistance with social media or CRM stuff, I'm a little bit more wary about it.

**Helen:** So you don't find the results — did social media ever bring you any real clients?

**Natalie:** I would not say so, no, not yet. But I've also only been doing it for — I mean barely a year and a half. I started like August 2024. It took like six months to do my first deal, or like four months.

## Part 3: 最大痛点

**Helen:** Let's move on. In the previous transactions, which part gives you the most headache — brings you the most trouble? Would you mind telling us what exactly happened?

**Natalie:** Oh yeah, probably the hardest — well, I do list side and buy side deals, but majority of them are buyer deals. And whenever we do inspections and the buyer wants to ask for a million things to get fixed — like, that's probably the most stressful part for me. I lose sleep.

Because they want everything fixed. Real estate is an "as-is" sale. So when somebody purchases a house, they're buying it as-is. However, if something's wrong with their HVAC or the systems in the house, oftentimes they'll request for that to be repaired. Sometimes it's $15,000, and then the seller says no.

And that to me can be the most taxing aspect of the whole deal — because then the buyer wants more, the seller is not willing to give as much, and you have to help them find an agreement somewhere in the middle.

A lot of buyers think they can get whatever they want now. Like "oh, I'm buying the house, I want everything brand new." And it doesn't work that way.

## Part 4: 最想跳过的工作

**Helen:** If you have to choose, which three hours of work would you like to skip most? And why?

**Natalie:** Probably just having to keep up with the CRM and emailing people. Like, once I have somebody that's communicating with me, I like to email them. But like the "Merry Christmas" or "it's spring now" — you know, just to get your face in front of them — I think that part is kind of annoying. I don't really like doing that.

## Part 5: 获客与付费意愿

**Natalie:** For any agent, getting the leads is definitely one of the most difficult parts. I know a lot of people go six months to a year without doing a deal because they just don't have the client. They might provide good service. That's why I was in a financial position where I could actually take that chance. And at first it was scary, but because I've seen the return, I continue to do that.

But if there was a way to generate more leads or to get my face in front of more people, that would be great. But in a perfect world, right?

**Helen:** What are you guys doing? Are you going to share some results with me?

**Natalie:** Oh, we are preparing to do some AI tools for realtors — to help realtors get leads or some transaction management. So you can understand that we are trying to do some AI startup for realtors, and currently we are doing some investigation to understand the main pain points of your workflow.

**Natalie:** Yeah, I might be interested someday. It's just — in the real estate industry everything costs a lot. You would think — it's always in the thousands. It's rarely $50 to try this, or $100 or $150. It's always like $800 and up. And it can be hefty.

## Part 6: 当前支出与工具

**Neil:** How much are you spending per month for your different kinds of tools?

**Natalie:** Over $300 a month at least. Because Coldwell Banker — that's the brokerage I work for — they're the most expensive one. Just to hang your license there, it costs a lot. Like Windermere is like $80 a month, Intero like $50 a month. Mine is $250 because they're the snobby, upper-class company.

## Part 7: Transaction Manager

**Julian:** If there is such a tool that can help you manage the transactions and also help you get leads — how much are you willing to spend?

**Natalie:** It depends. Because there's transaction managers that help me with the actual deal when I have a deal pending. They do not come into the picture until you have a ratified contract. And they assist — and I have a lady that does that. She assists me, we talk on the phone sometimes. She's like **$200 a transaction**. But she doesn't charge if the deal falls through.

But she doesn't help on the regular when I don't have a deal going — like with assisting with potential clients and the CRM.

**Julian:** How much do you pay for the CRM?

**Natalie:** I don't pay for it. It's through — that's probably part of the $250 for Coldwell Banker. But if I leave Coldwell Banker, they have all my — if I leave, my CRM is gone.

**Julian:** They are smart — they tie you up, right?

**Natalie:** That's how they tie you up, yes. And then you don't know how to use them very well either.

**Helen:** Transaction manager is to help you do the paperwork, right?

**Natalie:** Yeah, she does. But I'm fairly good at it on my own. **It's more staying up with — I would be much obliged to having somebody help me with emails and contacts, if I saw a return from one of those.** But I have not yet to see that. Everything I've seen a return for — strangers, basically. Most of my clients are strangers.

## Part 8: 总结问题

**Neil:** Natalie, if you had to describe your work of being an agent in a nutshell — what is the most painful thing in your opinion?

**Natalie:** I wouldn't necessarily say it's painful — maybe like annoying or a nuisance. That's a hard one. Maybe just the stress during negotiating. But I don't mind that — I like it. It just can get a little stressful when — clients, if they don't get what they want, they have emotions because it's their money that they're spending. And sometimes they need to direct that somewhere, and sometimes that's directed towards the agent.

But I haven't had a painful experience. I like it. But I also work another job, so I have two sources of income.

## Part 9: 中美房产市场对比

**Neil:** In China there's a very huge real estate company which maybe 70% or more agents are in that company. But it does not happen in the US, right? Why isn't there a very huge company like that?

**Natalie:** I wouldn't mind that, if we were all with one company.

**Neil:** You wouldn't mind?

**Natalie:** No, I wouldn't mind. Because then we're all paying the same thing.
