# 用户访谈记录 — Prita（房产经纪人）

**日期**: 2026年（具体日期待确认）
**时长**: ~32分钟
**参与者**:

- **Helen（说话人A/C）**: 访谈主持人
- **Prita（说话人B）**: 受访经纪人
- **Neil（说话人C/D）**: 产品开发者

---

## 第一部分：自我介绍与职业背景（0:00-5:00）

**Helen**: Hi, Prita. So today we would like to ask you a couple of questions. Would you please introduce a bit of yourself? How many years have you been a real estate agent? How many deals you close on average in a year?

**Prita**: This is my 10th year in real estate. I started in 2016. I'm not a very aggressive agent. Some years I'm motivated, some years I fall off the wagon. When my son was in college out of state, I was making like 200-300K per year. When he graduated, I slacked off for one or two years. But this year I bought a Mercedes Benz in December and said, now I need to be focused to pay off the car.

This year I'm doing really well. I have four listings going live in the next month, three or four lease listings going live, closed two buyer deals this year, got one offer accepted last night, and have two other buyers lined up. It's been a full plate in just two months.

**Helen**: Congratulations! In terms of clients, what are the major resources? Are your clients mainly from leads, social media, or third-party platforms?

**Prita**: My business is just referral, completely. Once a client has worked with me, it never goes to anybody else. Irrespective of how many years, they always come back to me and have given me a lot of leads. My clients themselves have given me the leads to keep my business rolling.

---

## 第二部分：营销投入与获客（5:00-10:00）

**Helen**: Do you spend any money on marketing or branding yourself?

**Prita**: Initially in my first two or three years, I spent a lot of money for branding, mailers and all that stuff. I know you have to do it consistently for a long time to see results. I did it for two or three years, whatever was recommended by my company at that time — this was 2016-17 when Facebook or Instagram wasn't that great as a platform. I did not see the ROI I expected. So I just focused on my clients and giving them the best service, and going above and beyond. That has given me the best ROI.

**Helen**: But where did your first client come from?

**Prita**: Open house. I consistently do open houses because if I get in front of people, that's my best thing. People quickly relate to me. The lead I got yesterday was somebody I had met at a housewarming party at my client's house last February. They saw an open house on Sunday, liked the house, called me, I wrote an offer, and we got it accepted last night. Getting in front of people has always worked for me.

---

## 第三部分：社交媒体尝试与痛点（10:00-18:00）

**Helen**: Since social media is really trending now, have you ever tried any of these? Are you planning to?

**Prita**: I tried to post on Instagram but I'm not good at it. I was planning to go to a new home subdivision today and make a reel, but my deal got accepted yesterday so I have to go for home inspection. I fall off the wagon pretty quickly. But if I see ROI, I would be interested. I'm Indian — we are Asians, we are end-result oriented. The patience is not my strong point. I need to see results. If you say yes, I'm ready to spend if I get good results and grab new leads.

**Helen**: Which part of the process of posting a video annoys you the most?

**Prita**: I need to go to the subdivision, dress up, need somebody to film me. I prefer someone else films me from a distance where I'm walking and showing things or having a conversation with the salesperson, rather than a selfie stick. I want people to see the space around me. So I need a friend or colleague to film me. Then I have to be dressed up, know what to say, have my script ready, edit the video. All this is time consuming. And I'm not very tech savvy, so it takes me a lot more time. That's my hurdle. I don't say I don't like it, it's just more time consuming for an Instagram post that's not even going to last that long.

**Helen**: If we offer two options: Option 1 — video with yourself in it but takes 3x the time/effort; Option 2 — video without yourself, finished within seconds and posted immediately. Which would you choose?

**Prita**: I would choose the second. I was very reluctant to come on camera. I feel I have a strong accent, I'm clumsy in front of the camera. But this year I decided to get over that fear because agents in my office who are in front of the camera are getting better results. But given a choice, I'd prefer the second option.

**Helen**: Have you tried posting videos without yourself?

**Prita**: Yes, I did that yesterday. Whenever I have a listing or I'm doing staging, I compile as I walk through. I do buyer diaries — I take videos of houses I preview and put my picture at the end saying "call me." I know that's very old fashioned, but I'm open to the idea. **I just need to know how much is it going to cost me.**

---

## 第四部分：日常工作痛点（18:00-22:00）

**Helen**: In your daily working routine, which part of the work costs you the most time?

**Prita**: It's my office paperwork. A lease listing is the most paperwork — you have to put it in MLS, then in another site for client verification, then in my office system. Then you post on different websites, Instagram, Facebook. It's just a lease listing but that's the kind of paperwork you have to do, there's no way around it. Even AI can't help you with that.

**Helen**: Apart from paperwork, what else tortures you the most?

**Prita**: That's it, really. I really like being in the house, seeing the house, explaining it, doing inspections, interacting with people. That's what I enjoy the most.

**Helen**: Neil is the main developer of a tool that helps formulate videos. Maybe Neil can introduce it.

**Prita**: Is it similar to CapCut?

**Neil**: No, it's much more intelligent. You talk with it like ChatGPT but it can produce videos.

**Prita**: And how much is the monthly subscription?

**Neil**: Currently free for trial. You can make maybe 10 or more videos totally free, then decide.

**Prita**: If I need help trying it, is there any training or someone to walk me through it? Because one thing is me trying, another is a tech person guiding me for best results.

**Neil**: We have a Telegram group. Helen, Clark and I will be in the group to help.

**Prita**: I'm open to this idea of trying because I have new listings coming up. Once I make the video, I'm going to post it on Instagram. So you're helping me with the video tool, not with my social media presence, right?

**Neil**: Right, helping with the video part. But if you need help with content, scripts, or text for social media, the AI can also help — like ChatGPT but much more powerful. We're developers, we can train the AI to help you post.

**Prita**: Which app do I download?

**Neil**: It's not an app. It's like adding a contact in WhatsApp and chatting with it.

**Prita**: How do I know this is secure? How do I know it's not spam or collecting my data?

**Helen**: It follows the same data restrictions as anyone else on WhatsApp. We can't cross the boundaries of WhatsApp's data security. We are not hackers.

**Prita**: See, a person who is capable is not going to tell it to my face, right? 😄 Okay, my same number is my WhatsApp.
